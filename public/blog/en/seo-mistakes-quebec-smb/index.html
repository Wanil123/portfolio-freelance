7 SEO mistakes costing Quebec SMBs customers

By Wanil Parfait·May 19, 2026·9 min read

Local SEO isn't witchcraft. But across 100+ Quebec SMB websites I've analyzed since 2024, almost all make the same 7 mistakes. Here's the exact checklist, in impact order, with how to fix each without rebuilding the site.

Mistake #1 — No Google Business profile (or abandoned)

In Quebec, 46% of local commercial searches go through Google Maps before the website. Without an active Google Business profile, you're invisible in those searches. You lose customers who'll never know you exist.

Fix:

  • Go to google.com/business
  • Create or claim your profile
  • Fill 100% of fields (hours, services, photos, description)
  • Ask 3-5 reviews from your best real clients
  • Post 1 update per month minimum

Impact: visible in 4-8 weeks. Often 2-3x your local leads.

Mistake #2 — Too generic keywords

Targeting "web developer"? You compete with 50,000 sites worldwide. Zero chance ranking. Target "web developer Montreal", "Shopify developer Laval", "SMB web agency Quebec" — specific and local.

Fix:

  • Identify 5-10 highly specific expressions for your niche + city
  • Rewrite titles and meta descriptions including them
  • Create one page per city/region if you serve multiple (Montreal, Laval, Longueuil, etc.)

Mistake #3 — Default title and meta description tags

"Home — My Site" as the homepage title. Worst possible title. Google uses this as your billboard in search results.

Optimal format:

  • Title (max 60 chars): "[Main benefit] | [City/Region] — [Brand name]"
  • Meta description (max 155 chars): one sentence answering the searcher's implicit question + a micro-CTA

Example: "Custom websites for Montreal SMBs — Delivered in 2-4 weeks | PrimeDev Studios". Clickable.

Mistake #4 — Slow site (especially mobile)

61% of traffic is mobile. Google harshly penalizes slow mobile sites since 2021. Mobile PageSpeed under 50 = invisible to Google on mobile.

Fix:

  • Test on pagespeed.web.dev
  • On WordPress: install WP Rocket + optimize images (WebP)
  • Score still under 70? Theme is probably too heavy. Change theme or migrate to custom (see WordPress vs custom).

Mistake #5 — No Schema.org local markup

Schema.org is invisible code telling Google "I'm a local business, here's my address, hours, category". Without it, Google guesses — often wrong.

Essential schema to add in your homepage <head>:

  • LocalBusiness or ProfessionalService (by type)
  • BreadcrumbList on internal pages
  • FAQPage on your FAQ page

Validate at search.google.com/test/rich-results.

Mistake #6 — No local and thematic content

Google rewards sites demonstrating expertise on a specific local topic. A "Home + Services + Contact" page set isn't enough. You need substantial text content.

What to write:

  • Minimum 1 blog article per month (like this one)
  • Topics targeting real customer questions
  • Natural mention of cities/regions you serve
  • 800-1,500 words per article — not fluff, real content

Visible effect: 3-6 months after publishing. Content SEO is long-term investment.

Mistake #7 — No tracking, so no improvement

If you don't know how many visitors you have, where they come from and what they do, you're flying blind. Every SEO decision becomes a guess.

Essential free tools:

  • Google Search Console: see traffic-driving keywords, spot indexing errors
  • Google Analytics OR Plausible (simpler, privacy-first): understand behavior
  • Microsoft Clarity (free): heatmaps and session recordings

Total setup: ~1hr. No reason not to do it.

Reality

Fix only 3 mistakes — #1 (Google Business), #4 (mobile speed), #7 (tracking) — and you'll likely see measurable improvement in 8-12 weeks. SEO rewards consistency over complexity.

The "guaranteed SEO" trap

Beware agencies promising "first page Google in 30 days". Technically impossible to guarantee — Google changes its algorithm constantly and competition varies. Any guaranteed ranking promise is a sales lie.

What's realistic: measurable 30-80% increase in organic traffic over 6-12 months, with sustained, honest work. No more, no less.
